About Debrigarh Sanctuary

Debrigarh Wildlife Sanctuary is located in the Bargarh district in the Indian state of Odisha, covering a total area of 346.91 km². It is situated near the city of Sambalpur's Hirakud Dam. The sanctuary is an important location for the conservation of various local wildlife and their habitat.

Geography

Location Map

The sanctuary is bound on the east and north by the huge Hirakud reservoir. It is one of the select few sanctuaries in the state supporting both terrestrial and aquatic biodiversity, which further attracts a significant number of migratory waterfowl during winter. The sanctuary is also home to over 250 plant species, many of which have ethno-botanical and medicinal value.

Debrigarh Wildlife Sanctuary is considered an Eco-sensitive Zone from both an ecological and environmental point of view, aiming to prohibit industries or certain class of industries and their operations in this area.

Wildlife

The sanctuary is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna. Over 40 species of mammals, 200 species of birds, 40 species of reptiles, 12 species of amphibians, 42 species of fishes, 39 species of odonates, 85 species of butterflies, and 38 species of spiders have been found living in the sanctuary.

Debrigarh is also home to a variety of wild animals, including cheetal, sambar deer, wild boar, Gaur, chowsingha, peacock, langur, bear, leopard, and Indian wild dog.

Key Attractions

Rich Biodiversity

Debrigarh Sanctuary is home to various species of flora and fauna, making it a paradise for wildlife enthusiasts.

Scenic Landscape

The sanctuary offers stunning views of rolling hills, lush green forests, and tranquil water bodies.

Adventure Activities

Visitors can enjoy activities like trekking, bird watching, and nature walks to immerse in the wilderness.

Flora & Fauna

The sanctuary is home to a variety of flora, including dense forests of sal, teak, and bamboo. The fauna includes a rich diversity of animals like tigers, leopards, sloth bears, and a wide variety of bird species. It’s also a critical habitat for endangered species.

Visitor Information

Debrigarh Sanctuary is open year-round, with peak tourist season during the cooler months. The sanctuary is accessible by road and is located about 40 km from Sambalpur city in Odisha, India.
